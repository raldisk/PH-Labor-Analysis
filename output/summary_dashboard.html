Counter-cyclicality confirmed

GDP–remittance growth correlation is not significantly positive. OFW transfers actively cushioned both the GFC 2009 (+8.9%) and COVID-19 2020 (−0.6%) shocks, consistent with remittances acting as a macro shock absorber.

Aug–Sep CPI seasonal spike

The dominant intra-year CPI pattern identified by STL decomposition. Driven by rice price dynamics and back-to-school expenditure. Predictable and persistent across the full 2000–2024 series.

BSP inflation target compliance

Monthly inflation exceeded the 2–4% target band in the majority of months — asymmetrically above the upper bound. Persistent above-target inflation is the structural baseline, not the exception.

Two structural shocks

GFC 2009 (GDP growth +1.1%) and COVID-19 2020 (−9.5%) are the two dominant break points in the GDP series. Both were followed by a recovery exceeding the pre-shock growth rate within 2 years.
